What your SDS Librarian can do for you

Support your research

We can work with you at all stages of your research lifecycle. We can help you with:

  • Acquiring and accessing resources for the library’s collection
  • Negotiating your rights as an author with your publishers
  • Preserving and disseminating your research outputs, and data
  • Complying with funding agencies' open access requirements
  • Determining the impact of your publications
  • Preparing to visit other university libraries
  • Developing, designing, and hosting your online research projects

Support your teaching and your students

  • I can teach your students the information skills they’ll need for your course. This can take the form of:
    • In-class presentations customized to your assignments
    • Online research guides, which can be customized and embedded in Quercus
    • Short instructional videos
    • Some of the topics that I can cover include:
      • How to get started on researching a topic
      • Searching effectively for scholarly sources using the library website and subject databases
      • Finding primary sources
      • Evaluating and choosing information sources
      • Citing sources and avoiding inadvertent plagiarism
      • Using Zotero to organize their sources and create citations
    • Office hours or drop-in sessions outside of class
    • One on one research consultations with your students
  • I can ensure that the library's collection has the right resources to support your assignments
  • Work with the Library's syllabus service team to make sure that your course readings are available in Quercus or on course reserves
  • Discuss incorporating Wikipedia, Wikidata, and exhibit projects into your assignments, as an alternative to other kinds of assignments

Contact me

Reach me via email or phone, or schedule a meeting. If I can’t help, I will refer you to the right person.
Jesse Carliner, Liaison Librarian for Sexual Diversity Studies
